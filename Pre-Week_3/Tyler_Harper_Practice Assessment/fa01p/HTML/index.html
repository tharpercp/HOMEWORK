Description

Shakshukah is a dish of eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, chili peppers, and onions, often spiced with cumin.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 medium brown onion
  • 1 garlic clove minced
  • 1 medium green or red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 cups ripe diced tomoatoes, or 2 cans(14 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper (or more depending on desired level of spiciness!)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 eggs
  • 1/2 tbsp fresh chopped parsley

Directions

  1. Heath olive oil in a deep large skillet or saute pan on medium.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened slightly (no more than a few minutes)
  3. Add the garlic and cook another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Add the bell pepper, and saute the mixture for 5-7 minutes until softened and opaque
  5. Add the tomatoes and tomato paste, stir to combine. Then add your spices and simmer for 5-7 minutes. Salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Crack the eggs one at a time over the tomato mixture, spacing evenly.
  7. Cover the pan, and allow to simmer another 10-15 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  8. Garnish with the chopped parsley, and enjoy!
